# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

People who encounter Alexander's work: potential collaborators, employers, and clients who want to understand what he builds and how he thinks; readers who follow his writing on Substack, Twitter, and LinkedIn; founders and operators who might want to work with him on a project. They arrive from a link in a bio, a shared post, or a search — they have 30 seconds to decide whether to stay.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio that does three things: shows Alexander's public writing in one place (linked out to the original platforms), presents his professional work as anonymized case studies, and introduces his side projects. The site is a credibility surface and a reading list, not a conversion funnel. Success means a visitor reads something, clicks through to a piece, or reaches out.

## Brand Personality

Editorial, curious, unhurried. The site should feel like a well-designed artifact — something made with care rather than assembled from a template. Warm without being expressive. Confident without being loud.

## Anti-references

- SaaS landing pages with metrics, testimonials, and CTAs
- LinkedIn-style "professional" portfolios with skill bars and certification badges
- Political or activist sites (the previous incarnation of this site)
- Agency portfolios with elaborate case study carousels
- Anything with the editorial-magazine aesthetic lane: display serif italics, drop caps, broadsheet grids, Klim-influenced typographic maximalism

## Design Principles

1. **Orbs carry the color.** Every section has a warm gradient orb as its visual anchor. The background is pure white. Color lives in the orbs, not the surface.
2. **Whitespace is the structure.** Generous empty space is not absence — it is what makes the orbs and the text legible. Resist filling it.
3. **Text earns its place.** Minimal copy per section. Every word is a deliberate choice. No section intros, no restatements.
4. **Motion is quiet.** Orbs breathe and drift slowly. Transitions are unhurried. Nothing pulses, bounces, or demands attention.
5. **Sections are distinct worlds.** Each section (Writing, Work, Projects) has its own orb color and feel. Consistency of voice, not sameness of treatment.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Reduced-motion alternative for all orb animations (static position, no drift). Body text contrast 4.5:1 minimum against white bg.
